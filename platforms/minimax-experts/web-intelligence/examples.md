## Verification Result: ⚠️ PARTIALLY ACCURATE / MISLEADING

**Accuracy Score:** 5/10
**Verdict:** The statistic is real but frequently misrepresented

---

## Source Tracing

### Original Source
- **Report:** World Economic Forum "Future of Jobs Report 2020"
- **Publisher:** World Economic Forum
- **Date:** October 2020
- **Full Quote:** "By 2025, the time spent on current tasks at work by humans and machines will be equal... 85 million jobs may be displaced by a shift in the division of labor between humans and machines"

### Key Context Missing

The original report actually stated:
1. **85 million displaced** BUT **97 million new roles** created
2. This represents a **net gain** of 12 million jobs
3. The focus was on **job transformation**, not just replacement
4. The 2025 timeline was a projection from 2020 data

---

## How the Statistic Is Misused

| Common Misrepresentation | Accurate Context |
|--------------------------|------------------|
| "AI will eliminate 85 million jobs" | Jobs will be displaced AND transformed |
| Implies net job loss | Report shows net job gain |
| Suggests mass unemployment | Focuses on skills transition |
| Presents as prediction | Presented as projection/scenario |
